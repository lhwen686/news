感光基因或连接三种儿童肿瘤类型

Light-sensing genes may connect three childhood tumor types

这项研究揭示了罕见小儿脑肿瘤松果体母细胞瘤的起源，并发现了一种跨多种中枢神经系统肿瘤（包括髓母细胞瘤和视网膜母细胞瘤）的共享分子依赖性。核心机制在于，研究人员利用单细胞分辨率技术，创建了正常松果体发育图谱，并将其与肿瘤细胞基因表达进行比对。他们发现，一组通常在松果体中参与光感受和光转导、与昼夜节律相关的“光感基因”在这些肿瘤中异常高表达，且对肿瘤的形成和生存至关重要，表明肿瘤对其存在“上瘾”现象。通过CRISPR基因编辑技术移除这些基因后，肿瘤细胞死亡，证实了这种共享的治疗依赖性。

这种“光感特征”的发现具有深远的行业影响。首先，它为理解多种儿童脑肿瘤的共同发育起源提供了新视角，即不同部位的肿瘤可能共享相似的分子基础。其次，它指明了一个新的治疗靶点，即针对这一共享的“光感特征”进行干预。这有望开发出针对松果体母细胞瘤、髓母细胞瘤和视网膜母细胞瘤等多种中枢神经系统恶性肿瘤的广谱靶向疗法，打破传统肿瘤治疗的器官局限性，提高罕见和侵袭性儿童脑肿瘤的治疗效果和患者预后。这种基于分子特征的精准治疗策略，预示着神经肿瘤学领域未来发展的重要方向。

Research uncovering the origin of pineoblastoma, a rare pediatric brain tumor, has also revealed a dependency across multiple brain tumor types that share a similar molecular program. Scientists at St. Jude Children's Research Hospital, Dana-Farber/Boston Children's Cancer and Blood Disorders Center, and Uppsala University have assembled and profiled the largest cohort of pineoblastoma tumors to date with single-cell resolution.

The researchers found a set of light-sensing-related genes essential to pineoblastoma formation in the developing pineal gland. They then extended the finding to medulloblastoma and retinoblastoma, indicating a common developmental state and a potential shared therapeutic dependency that could be targeted to improve their treatment. The findings are published in Cancer Cell.

"Pineoblastoma is extremely rare; St. Jude treats only a handful of cases each year," said corresponding and co-senior author Paul Northcott, Ph.D., St. Jude Center of Excellence in Neuro-Oncology Sciences (CENOS) director, Cancer Center Neurobiology and Brain Tumor Program co-leader and Department of Developmental Neurobiology member. "By collaborating with other institutions, we went much deeper than previous profiling efforts to understand where these tumors come from, how they overlap or differ at single-cell resolution, and what makes them vulnerable."

Illuminating a tumor origin

During early human development, brain cells rapidly expand, divide and migrate to form complex structures, such as the pineal gland. The pineal gland is a pinecone-shaped structure in the center of the brain that releases hormones, such as melatonin. The scientists believed that pineoblastomas arise when something goes wrong during the rapid development of the pineal gland.

However, before they could investigate how things could go wrong, they first had to understand how things go right during normal development. To do so, they created the first single-cell atlas of normal pineal gland development, including all the cell types involved.

Once the researchers had created their atlas, they measured gene expression in the cohort of rare tumor samples from 38 patients and compared it to normal pineal gland development.

By measuring gene expression using single-cell RNA-sequencing, they found that a population of early cells called pinealocyte progenitors is the most similar to these tumors, implicating their role in pineoblastoma development. They created unique mouse models through the perturbation of five different pineoblastoma driver genes in these progenitors. The five models all faithfully resembled corresponding human subtypes based on extensive molecular and histological analysis.

Despite the subtypes having different cancer drivers, they all shared one notable gene expression signature.

"We saw a recurring theme that was related to light sensitivity," Northcott said. "The pineal gland plays an important role in circadian rhythm and interpreting light from the retina, so it normally expresses a set of photoreceptor and phototransduction genes. Not only are these same genes expressed in pineoblastoma, but they are expressed at very high levels, suggesting the tumors might be 'addicted' to them."

Bringing a shared dependency into the light

"The light-sensing signature reminded us of a very similar program that we had seen in a subtype of another brain cancer, Group 3 medulloblastoma," Northcott said. "When we looked closer at other cancers, we saw the same genes, transcription factors and biomarkers of this program were shared between anatomically distinct central nervous system tumor types, including tumors of the pineal gland, retina and cerebellum."

This abnormally expressed light-sensing signature indicated that this set of genes may be essential for these cancers to form and survive, suggesting a potential cross-tumor vulnerability. The scientists used CRISPR to remove genes from this signature in pineoblastoma, medulloblastoma and retinoblastoma cells, to see if it affected their growth. They found all three cancers had a shared dependence on those genes, as tumors died without them.

"We found a subset of these light-sensing genes to have very strong selective dependencies in these particular cancer types," Northcott said. "With that information, we've opened the door to explore therapeutically targeting this shared signature across multiple brain tumor types in the future."

一项揭示罕见小儿脑肿瘤——松果体母细胞瘤起源的研究，也揭示了多种共享类似分子程序的脑肿瘤类型之间的依赖性。圣犹大儿童研究医院、丹娜—法伯/波士顿儿童癌症和血液疾病中心以及乌普萨拉大学的科学家们，迄今已组建并以单细胞分辨率分析了最大规模的松果体母细胞瘤队列。

研究人员发现了一组与光感相关的基因，它们对发育中的松果体形成松果体母细胞瘤至关重要。随后，他们将这一发现扩展到髓母细胞瘤和视网膜母细胞瘤，表明这些肿瘤存在共同的发育状态和潜在的共享治疗依赖性，可作为靶点以改善治疗效果。该研究成果发表在《癌细胞》杂志上。

“松果体母细胞瘤极为罕见；圣犹大医院每年仅治疗少数病例，”圣犹大神经肿瘤学卓越中心（CENOS）主任、癌症中心神经生物学和脑肿瘤项目共同负责人兼发育神经生物学系成员、通讯作者兼共同资深作者保罗·诺思科特博士说。“通过与其他机构合作，我们比以往的分析工作更深入地了解了这些肿瘤的来源、它们在单细胞分辨率下的重叠或差异，以及它们的脆弱性所在。”

阐明肿瘤起源

在人类早期发育过程中，脑细胞快速增殖、分裂和迁移，形成复杂结构，例如松果体。松果体位于大脑中央，呈松果状，负责释放褪黑素等激素。科学家们认为，当松果体快速发育过程中出现问题时，就会产生松果体母细胞瘤。

然而，在他们能够调查问题如何发生之前，他们首先必须了解正常发育过程中事情是如何正确进行的。为此，他们创建了第一个正常松果体发育的单细胞图谱，涵盖了所有相关细胞类型。

研究人员创建图谱后，测量了来自38名患者的罕见肿瘤样本队列中的基因表达，并将其与正常松果体发育进行比较。

通过使用单细胞RNA测序测量基因表达，他们发现一群被称为松果体细胞祖细胞的早期细胞与这些肿瘤最为相似，暗示了它们在松果体母细胞瘤发展中的作用。他们通过扰动这些祖细胞中五种不同的松果体母细胞瘤驱动基因，创建了独特的动物模型。基于广泛的分子和组织学分析，这五种模型都忠实地再现了相应的人类亚型。

尽管这些亚型具有不同的癌症驱动因素，但它们都共享一个显著的基因表达特征。

“我们看到了一个与光敏感性相关的反复出现的主题，”诺思科特说。“松果体在昼夜节律和解析来自视网膜的光线中发挥着重要作用，因此它通常会表达一组光感受器和光转导基因。这些相同的基因不仅在松果体母细胞瘤中表达，而且表达水平非常高，这表明肿瘤可能对其‘上瘾’。”

揭示共享依赖性

“这种光感特征让我们想起了我们在另一种脑癌——3组髓母细胞瘤的一个亚型中看到的非常相似的程序，”诺思科特说。“当我们更仔细地观察其他癌症时，我们发现这个程序的相同基因、转录因子和生物标志物在解剖学上不同的中枢神经系统肿瘤类型之间是共享的，包括松果体、视网膜和小脑的肿瘤。”

这种异常表达的光感特征表明，这组基因可能对这些癌症的形成和生存至关重要，暗示了潜在的跨肿瘤脆弱性。科学家们使用CRISPR技术从松果体母细胞瘤、髓母细胞瘤和视网膜母细胞瘤细胞中移除了该特征中的基因，以观察是否影响其生长。他们发现，这三种癌症都对这些基因存在共同依赖性，因为没有这些基因，肿瘤细胞就会死亡。

“我们发现这些光感基因中的一个子集在这些特定的癌症类型中具有非常强的选择性依赖性，”诺思科特说。“有了这些信息，我们为未来探索在多种脑肿瘤类型中治疗性靶向这一共享特征打开了大门。”
