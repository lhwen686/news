Original Text
In April, 2026, the Sudanese people will mark 3 years of ongoing conflict. Sudan remains the world's largest displacement crisis, with 13·6 million people experiencing diverse patterns of displacement, including 3·3 million who have returned home, whether voluntarily or under coercive circumstances.1 Although international reporting, including the Lancet's World Report,2 has largely focused on documenting atrocities, famine, and the collapse of the country's national health system, the growing wave of returnees to areas lacking even basic functioning infrastructure has remained almost entirely invisible.
