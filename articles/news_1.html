Original Text
In February, 2026, the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) Blood Panel convened in Brussels, Belgium, to update policies for adequate blood supply for future conflicts and health crises. Although our focus was military support, we recommend civilian blood services assess their ability to deliver timely, life‑saving transfusions during major surges in demand. In this Correspondence, we present the rationale for this recommendation and the actions we believe are required.
