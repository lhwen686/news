Original Text
Aspirin is considered the cornerstone of secondary prevention of coronary artery disease. However, the evidence supporting aspirin was largely generated before the advent of modern pharmacological and interventional therapies.1 Consequently, its primacy has increasingly been questioned, particularly considering accumulating evidence comparing P2Y12 inhibitors versus aspirin as antiplatelet monotherapy. By binding to the adenosine diphosphate platelet receptor, clopidogrel induces broader platelet inhibition than aspirin while not interfering with the protective role of prostaglandins on the gastric mucosa.
