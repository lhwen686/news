Shisha smoking continues to be overlooked as a public health issue in the UK, argue experts in The BMJ. This could change with the advent of the Tobacco and Vapes Bill, legislation currently making its way through Parliament, but only, they say, with more recognition and culture-specific campaigns and support in affected communities.

Shisha (also known as hookah, narghile, and hubble-bubble) is a waterpipe that is used to smoke a charcoal-heated tobacco mix. With almost 1,000 shisha cafes in the UK—and more in London than there are branches of McDonald's—the practice is popular, especially among young people and some ethnic minority groups.

Yet, unlike cigarettes, shisha tobacco does not have the benefit of decades of regulation.

Its exclusion from flavor bans, for example, and a lack of licensing for businesses selling shisha tobacco is evidence of this "underregulation," says Mohammed Jawad, a public health consultant and specialist in shisha tobacco smoking.

Hazel Cheeseman from Action on Smoking and Health says shisha is "every bit as bad for you as smoking cigarettes, and the flavors make it appear less harmful and make it more palatable … it's an inequality not to apply the law equally."

Leena Ali at the University of York also points out that shisha-specific research in the UK is about a decade old, and although shisha is popular among people from Middle Eastern backgrounds, these groups are not accurately represented in the data, which hinders accurate monitoring.

This lack of knowledge, research, and awareness of shisha affects policy.

Jawad warns that shisha is not just another form of smoking and therefore covered by existing tobacco laws. Consumption patterns differ, with single sessions lasting up to an hour, delivering "quite a lot of toxins" compared to an occasional cigarette smoker, he explains. Unique design features, apparatus sharing, and prolonged exposure also change the risk profile.

The upcoming bill may make it easier to tackle the problem by creating consistency across all tobacco products, including regulating flavored tobacco and licensing. However, Ali expresses concern about the bill's lack of focus on shisha, questioning whether funding will be allocated to it and whether the bill's age of sale increases could drive shisha use into homes, potentially increasing its use.

With the bill not set to come into force until 2027, experts also emphasize the need for work that can be done now, including culturally tailored health messaging.

Jawad notes a lack of national anti-smoking campaigns mentioning shisha, which increases the likelihood of misinformation. He suggests funding community-led research, framing it as a "health equity issue, rather than a cultural attack, which is how it's currently being perceived."

Local councils, like Bradford, are starting to conduct local research to target awareness efforts to specific communities where shisha prevalence is highest.

《英国医学杂志》的专家们指出，水烟吸食在英国作为一个公共卫生问题持续被忽视。随着《烟草与电子烟法案》的出现，这种情况可能会有所改变，该法案目前正在议会审议中，但专家们表示，只有在受影响社区获得更多认可并开展针对特定文化的宣传活动和支持后，才可能实现这一转变。

水烟（亦称 hookah、narghile 和 hubble-bubble）是一种水烟筒，用于吸食木炭加热的烟草混合物。英国有近 1000 家水烟馆——伦敦的水烟馆数量甚至超过了麦当劳的分店——这种做法非常流行，尤其是在年轻人和一些少数民族群体中。

然而，与香烟不同，水烟烟草尚未受益于数十年的监管。

公共卫生顾问兼水烟吸食专家 Mohammed Jawad 表示，例如，水烟被排除在口味禁令之外，以及销售水烟烟草的企业缺乏许可，都证明了这种“监管不足”。

英国吸烟与健康行动组织（Action on Smoking and Health）的 Hazel Cheeseman 表示，水烟“对你造成的危害与吸烟香烟完全相同，而且其口味使其看起来危害较小，更易接受……不平等地适用法律是一种不公平。”

约克大学的 Leena Ali 也指出，英国针对水烟的特定研究已有大约十年之久，尽管水烟在中东背景人群中很受欢迎，但这些群体在数据中并未得到准确体现，这阻碍了准确的监测。

这种对水烟的知识、研究和意识的缺乏影响了政策制定。

Jawad 警告说，水烟不仅仅是另一种吸烟形式，因此不能简单地被现有烟草法律涵盖。他解释说，水烟的消费模式不同，单次吸食可长达一小时，与偶尔吸烟的人相比，会输送“相当多的毒素”。独特的设计特征、器具共享和长时间暴露也改变了其风险概况。

即将出台的法案可能通过对所有烟草产品（包括调味烟草和许可）实行一致性监管，从而更容易解决这个问题。然而，Ali 对该法案缺乏对水烟的关注表示担忧，她质疑是否会为其分配资金，以及法案提高销售年龄的规定是否会将水烟使用推向家庭，从而可能增加其使用量。

鉴于该法案要到 2027 年才生效，专家们还强调了现在可以开展的工作，包括针对文化特点的健康宣传。

Jawad 指出，国家戒烟宣传活动中缺乏提及水烟的内容，这增加了错误信息的可能性。他建议资助由社区主导的研究，将其定义为“健康公平问题，而不是目前被认为的文化攻击”。

布拉德福德等地方议会正在开始进行地方研究，以便将宣传工作瞄准水烟流行率最高的特定社区。
