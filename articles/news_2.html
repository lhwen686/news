Original Text
The disparity in global childhood cancer survival is well known: survival probabilities exceed 80% in high-income countries, whereas they remain around 30% in low-income countries.1–3 But there are still gaps in our knowledge in this area, among them the ability to measure and compare progress across diverse health-care settings worldwide to guide strategic investments and accelerate further improvements in survival. The Lancet Oncology Commission estimated that approximately 13·7 million children will develop cancer worldwide between 2020 and 2050, with about 6·1 million likely to remain undiagnosed under current health-system performance.
