宠物猫对人类癌症的启示

What pet cats can tell us about human cancer

这项研究揭示了跨物种比较肿瘤学的重要潜力，特别是在家猫癌症与人类癌症之间的遗传相似性方面。文章指出，猫和人类癌症在TP53等抑癌基因和PIK3CA等致癌基因上存在高度同源的突变模式，这为利用针对人类癌症突变的现有药物来治疗猫提供了可能性，从而加速了药物再利用的进程。更重要的是，猫可以作为某些在人类中罕见但更具侵袭性癌症（如三阴性乳腺癌和腺泡型胰腺癌）的自然发生模型。相较于基因工程改造的啮齿动物模型，猫的癌症是在与人类相似的环境中自发形成的，并伴有多种并发症，使其成为一个更具生理相关性的研究平台。这不仅能促进对这些难治性人类癌症生物学的深入理解，也可能为开发更有效、转化成功率更高的抗癌疗法提供新途径，从而惠及人类和宠物患者。

They live in our houses, drink our water and even sleep in our beds. Cats have become an integral part of many households and share much of our lives. They also share much of their biology with humans. Pet cats get cancer at a rate similar to humans and often develop the same types of cancer. Just like in humans, as health care and diets have improved, cats are living longer, which puts them at a higher lifetime risk of cancer.

But how similar are cat cancers to human cancers at the genetic level? Research colleagues and I have conducted the largest-ever cancer DNA sequencing study of cat tumors. Our research reveals striking similarities between feline and human cancers, and the results reveal benefits for cats as well as humans.

Newly published work from our international collaboration studied the tumors of 500 cats, including 13 different tumor types. We isolated DNA from these tumors, and mapped the sequence of 1,000 genes that are often found mutated in human cancers.

Cat and human cancers

Overall, the most commonly mutated gene was a cancer protective gene called TP53, which is also the most commonly mutated gene in human cancers. Another example is the gene PIK3CA, which is mutated in about 40% of human breast cancers and was found to be altered in about 50% of cat mammary cancers.

There are drugs that have been specifically developed to work on human cancers with certain mutations like those in PIK3CA. Now that we know what mutations are common in cat cancers, there is an opportunity to test these drugs for treating cats.

How do we study cancer in cats? Since 2009, the Ontario Veterinary College's Veterinary Biobank, part of the Institute for Comparative Cancer Investigation at the University of Guelph, has been banking samples of tumors from cats treated at the Animal Cancer Center.

With owner consent, part of a cat's tumor that is already being removed during surgery is saved and frozen for future studies. Also banked are blood samples, which serve as a resource for developing more non-invasive cancer tests using cancer-associated molecules found in blood.

Recently, the Veterinary Biobank has joined the Ontario Biobanks consortium of human biobanks to help facilitate more cross-species cancer studies. In addition, cancer clinical trials are being conducted in cat and dog patients to help translate research findings into better outcomes for pets with cancer, and to better inform us about human cancers as well.

Cats can potentially teach us quite a lot about human cancer. There are several cancers or cancer subtypes that are common in cats but rare in humans. "Triple negative" breast cancer—which lacks estrogen receptors, progesterone receptors and a growth factor receptor called HER2—is by far the most common subtype in cats. However, it accounts for only 15% of human breast cancers.

This subtype tends to occur in younger women, Black women and women with an inherited genetic predisposition (BRCA1 gene mutation) and is particularly aggressive and hard to treat.

Another example is pancreatic cancer. The acinar subtype that cats get most commonly is relatively rare in humans. Studying these rare human subtypes is potentially easier to do in cats as there are more cases.

Our cat sequencing study also found a few differences in mutation patterns between cat and human cancers. About 25% of human cancers have mutations in RAS genes, whereas RAS mutations are rare in cat cancers. Studying these cancers in cats can help us to understand the biology of RAS genes in cancer.

Cat and mouse genomes

Cancer charities and agencies that provide grants to study human health routinely support studies that use rodent models of human cancer, but studying cancer in other species has been a harder sell.

Rodent models are either genetically engineered to develop cancer or are engineered to have a severely deficient immune system so that they can host human cancer cells.

These models are very powerful for examining the molecular mechanisms of cancer but have a poor track record for developing cancer drugs. More than 90% of new cancer treatments developed using mouse models fail in human cancer trials and are never approved for clinical use.

In stark contrast, cat cancers frequently develop spontaneously in the same environment as humans. They also often have many of the same underlying or co-occurring medical conditions as humans, such as obesity, autoimmune diseases, kidney disease, diabetes and various other endocrine disorders.

Cat and human genomes are much more similar than mouse and human genomes, and the organization of cat genomes (the order of genes on the chromosomes) is much closer between cats and humans than between dogs and humans.

The (human) Cancer Genome Atlas is a massive open-access resource of mutations found in different types of cancer. Until now, no such resource existed for cats.

The data from our recent publication are available through the Wellcome Sanger Institute and will serve as a fundamental—and free—resource for researchers studying cancer in cats and humans for the benefit of both species.

它们住在我们的房子里，喝着我们的水，甚至睡在我们的床上。猫已经成为许多家庭不可或缺的一部分，并与我们分享了生活中的许多方面。它们也与人类分享了许多生物学特性。家猫患癌症的比例与人类相似，并且常患同类型的癌症。正如人类一样，随着医疗保健和饮食的改善，猫的寿命也越来越长，这使得它们一生中患癌症的风险更高。

但猫的癌症在基因层面与人类癌症有多大相似性呢？我和我的研究同事们进行了有史以来规模最大的猫肿瘤癌症DNA测序研究。我们的研究揭示了猫科动物和人类癌症之间惊人的相似之处，研究结果对猫和人类都有益。

我们国际合作团队最新发表的研究成果分析了500只猫的肿瘤，其中包括13种不同的肿瘤类型。我们从这些肿瘤中分离出DNA，并对1000个常在人类癌症中发现突变的基因进行了测序。

猫与人类癌症

总体而言，最常见的突变基因是一种名为TP53的抑癌基因，它也是人类癌症中最常见的突变基因。另一个例子是PIK3CA基因，该基因在大约40%的人类乳腺癌中发生突变，在约50%的猫乳腺癌中也发现其发生改变。

目前已有专门针对人类癌症中某些突变（如PIK3CA突变）而开发的药物。既然我们已知哪些突变在猫癌症中很常见，便有机会测试这些药物是否能用于治疗猫。

我们如何研究猫的癌症？自2009年以来，安大略兽医学院的兽医生物样本库（圭尔夫大学比较癌症研究中心的一部分）一直在收集在动物癌症中心接受治疗的猫的肿瘤样本。

在获得主人同意的情况下，手术中已切除的猫肿瘤的一部分会被保存并冷冻，用于未来的研究。同时，血液样本也会被储存起来，作为开发利用血液中发现的癌症相关分子进行更少侵入性癌症检测的资源。

最近，兽医生物样本库已加入安大略人类生物样本库联盟，以促进更多的跨物种癌症研究。此外，目前正在猫狗患者中进行癌症临床试验，以帮助将研究结果转化为患癌宠物的更好预后，并为我们提供更多关于人类癌症的信息。

猫有可能教会我们很多关于人类癌症的知识。有几种癌症或癌症亚型在猫身上很常见，但在人类身上却很少见。“三阴性”乳腺癌——缺乏雌激素受体、孕激素受体和HER2（人表皮生长因子受体2）生长因子受体——是迄今为止猫中最常见的亚型。然而，它仅占人类乳腺癌的15%。

这种亚型倾向于发生在年轻女性、黑人女性以及有遗传性基因易感性（BRCA1基因突变）的女性中，且特别具有侵袭性，难以治疗。

另一个例子是胰腺癌。猫最常患的腺泡亚型在人类中相对罕见。由于猫的病例较多，在猫身上研究这些罕见的人类亚型可能更容易。

我们的猫测序研究还发现猫和人类癌症之间在突变模式上存在一些差异。大约25%的人类癌症有RAS基因突变，而RAS突变在猫癌症中很少见。研究猫身上的这些癌症可以帮助我们理解RAS基因在癌症中的生物学作用。

猫和小鼠基因组

为人类健康研究提供资助的癌症慈善机构和组织通常支持使用啮齿动物模型进行人类癌症研究，但在其他物种中研究癌症一直较难被接受。

啮齿动物模型要么经过基因工程改造使其患癌，要么经过改造使其免疫系统严重缺陷，以便能够宿主人类癌细胞。

这些模型在研究癌症分子机制方面非常强大，但在开发癌症药物方面的记录不佳。90%以上使用小鼠模型开发的新型癌症疗法在人类癌症临床试验中失败，从未获批用于临床。

相比之下，猫的癌症经常在与人类相同的环境中自然发生。它们也常常患有与人类相同的许多潜在或共患疾病，例如肥胖症、自身免疫性疾病、肾脏疾病、糖尿病和各种其他内分泌疾病。

猫和人类的基因组比小鼠和人类的基因组相似得多，而且猫基因组的组织方式（染色体上基因的排列顺序）在猫和人类之间比在狗和人类之间更接近。

（人类）癌症基因组图谱是一个大规模的开放获取资源，收录了不同类型癌症中发现的突变。在此之前，猫尚无此类资源。

我们最近发表的数据可通过维康桑格研究所获取，并将成为研究猫和人类癌症研究人员的基本（且免费）资源，造福这两个物种。
