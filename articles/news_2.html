A new biomarker helps assess the aggressiveness of glioblastoma

Researchers from the Biomedical Data Science Laboratory (BDSLab) at the ITACA Institute of the Universitat Politècnica de València have developed a new method based on magnetic resonance imaging that enables objective quantification of the growth of the most aggressive brain tumors, particularly glioblastoma. The study, published in the journal Medical Physics, addresses one of the main clinical challenges in the diagnosis and treatment of this tumor: its high capacity to infiltrate healthy brain tissue.

In their work, the UPV's BDSLab team presents a new biomarker, the Dynamic Infiltration Rate (DIR), capable of identifying different patterns of tumor growth and independently predicting patient survival.

"Until now, assessment methods have mainly been based on measuring the increase in tumor size or the displacement of brain structures, without adequately capturing how the tumor grows or its biomechanical impact on the surrounding brain," explains Carles López Mateu, lead author of the study.

The research was carried out by Carles López Mateu, María Gómez Mahiques, F. Javier Gil Terrón, Víctor Montosa i Micó, Juan M. García-Gómez and Elies Fuster García, in collaboration with researchers from Oslo University Hospital.

Brain biomechanics

The biomarker developed by the BDSLab-ITACA team combines the tumor's volumetric growth over time with the mechanical effects this growth exerts on adjacent brain tissue. From a longitudinal analysis of magnetic resonance imaging, the researchers have generated tissue compression maps that allow them to assess how the tumor compresses or infiltrates healthy tissue.

The DIR biomarker integrates both phenomena and allows differentiation between more proliferative tumors that compress the brain and more infiltrative tumors that expand without significant compression.

"This index allows us to characterize the biological behavior of the tumor beyond its size and provides key information on its aggressiveness," notes Carles López.

The method has been validated using both synthetic data and two international clinical cohorts of glioblastoma patients. The results show that the DIR allows for robust stratification of patients according to their prognosis.

"Patients with low DIR values have an average survival of 35.2 weeks, compared to 16.0 weeks for those with high values," highlights María Gómez Mahiques, a researcher at ITACA and co-author of the study.

These results demonstrate the potential of the DIR as a tool to support clinical decision-making by facilitating a more precise characterization of tumor aggressiveness.

Towards more personalized medicine

The study conducted by the UPV team and Oslo University Hospital paves the way for more personalized medicine by enabling therapeutic strategies and follow-up protocols to be tailored to the growth pattern of each tumor.

"This is a quantitative, reproducible and non-invasive biomarker, based exclusively on medical imaging, which reinforces the role of biomedical engineering and data science in precision oncology and, at the same time, utilizes accessible methodologies that facilitate its future transfer to clinical practice," the authors conclude.

瓦伦西亚理工大学 ITACA 研究所生物医学数据科学实验室 (BDSLab) 的研究人员开发了一种基于磁共振成像的新方法，该方法能够客观量化最具侵袭性脑肿瘤（特别是胶质母细胞瘤）的生长。这项研究发表在《医学物理学》杂志上，旨在解决这种肿瘤在诊断和治疗方面的主要临床挑战之一：其强大的浸润健康脑组织的能力。

在他们的工作中，瓦伦西亚理工大学 BDSLab 团队提出了一种新的生物标志物——动态浸润率 (DIR)，它能够识别不同的肿瘤生长模式，并独立预测患者生存期。

研究的主要作者 Carles López Mateu 解释说：“到目前为止，评估方法主要基于测量肿瘤大小的增加或脑结构的位移，未能充分捕捉肿瘤的生长方式或其对周围大脑的生物力学影响。”

这项研究由 Carles López Mateu、María Gómez Mahiques、F. Javier Gil Terrón、Víctor Montosa i Micó、Juan M. García-Gómez 和 Elies Fuster García 与奥斯陆大学医院的研究人员合作完成。

脑生物力学

由 BDSLab-ITACA 团队开发的这种生物标志物，结合了肿瘤随时间变化的体积增长及其对相邻脑组织产生的机械效应。通过对磁共振成像进行纵向分析，研究人员生成了组织压缩图，从而评估肿瘤如何压缩或浸润健康组织。

DIR 生物标志物整合了这两种现象，能够区分更具增殖性、压迫大脑的肿瘤与更具浸润性、无显著压迫地扩张的肿瘤。

Carles López 指出：“该指标使我们能够超越肿瘤大小，表征其生物学行为，并提供关于其侵袭性的关键信息。”

该方法已通过合成数据和两个国际胶质母细胞瘤患者临床队列进行验证。结果表明，DIR 能够根据患者预后进行稳健分层。

ITACA 研究所研究员、该研究的共同作者 María Gómez Mahiques 强调：“低 DIR 值的患者平均生存期为 35.2 周，而高 DIR 值的患者则为 16.0 周。”

这些结果证明了 DIR 作为一种工具的潜力，它通过促进对肿瘤侵袭性的更精确表征来支持临床决策。

迈向更个性化的医疗

瓦伦西亚理工大学团队和奥斯陆大学医院进行的这项研究，通过使治疗策略和随访方案能够根据每个肿瘤的生长模式进行调整，为更个性化的医疗铺平了道路。

作者总结道：“这是一种定量、可重复且非侵入性的生物标志物，完全基于医学影像，它强化了生物医学工程和数据科学在精准肿瘤学中的作用，同时利用了可及的方法学，便于其未来向临床实践转化。”
