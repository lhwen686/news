Original Text
Venous thromboembolism includes deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and pulmonary embolism, which have incidence rates of between 1 and 2 cases per 1000 people for the first episode.1,2 Strong, provoking risk factors for venous thromboembolism include major surgery and active cancer, but in young, otherwise healthy patients, these factors are uncommon and usually prompt a thorough search for inherited or acquired thrombophilia.3 When thrombosis recurs in the absence of identifiable risk factors, local anatomical causes should be considered.
