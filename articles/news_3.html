Original Text
Gun violence is characterised by fatalities, severe injuries, psychological stress, and mental health conditions, all of which inflict profound grief and economic losses on survivors, families, and communities at large.1,2 In 2023, more than 268 000 people died from firearm injuries and 72% of these deaths took place in countries classified as low and middle on the Socio-demographic Index.3,4 Globally, males experience a higher injury burden of physical violence by firearms than females, especially in Latin America.
