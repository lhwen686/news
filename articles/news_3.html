Original Text
The devastation caused by COVID-19 brought pandemic prevention, preparedness, and response (PPPR) to the forefront of the global political agenda. But in many quarters, global attention has waned amid inflation, war, and challenges tied to the proliferation of artificial intelligence, even as disease threats such as mpox and avian influenza A(H5N1) persist.1 Fortunately, the UN General Assembly had already agreed to hold a follow-up High-Level Meeting (HLM) on PPPR in 2026 to review implementation of its 2023 Political Declaration on PPPR2 and consider the amendments to the International Health Regulations (IHR), which were adopted in 2024,3 and the 2025 WHO Pandemic Agreement4 as related processes.
