Clot buster may stop promising stroke medicine from working properly

这篇医学新闻揭示了缺血性卒中治疗中的一个关键药物相互作用问题。核心机制在于，目前用于急性缺血性卒中溶栓的标准药物组织型纤溶酶原激活剂（tPA）在发挥其溶解血栓作用时，会生成一种名为纤溶酶的酶。研究发现，这种纤溶酶能够分解另一种有潜力的新型抗炎药物——白细胞介素-1受体拮抗剂（IL-1Ra，即阿那白滞素）。当IL-1Ra与tPA同时给药时，由于IL-1Ra被纤溶酶降解，其抗炎作用减弱，同时也会显著削弱tPA对脑损伤的保护效果。相反，如果IL-1Ra在tPA之后给药，则不会发生这种负面相互作用，tPA的益处得以保留。

这一发现对卒中治疗领域具有深远影响。它强调了在开发新型辅助治疗方案时，必须严格评估其与现有标准护理药物的相互作用。临床实践中，这意味着未来IL-1Ra等抗炎药物若要应用于急性缺血性卒中，其给药时机至关重要，可能需要调整为序贯给药策略，即先给予溶栓药物，待其作用完成后再给予抗炎药物，以避免降低疗效甚至产生不利影响。此外，这项研究也提示需要探索其他溶栓药物（如替奈普酶），它们可能因其特异性更高而较少分解IL-1Ra，从而为联合治疗提供更多选择，最终有望优化缺血性卒中的临床管理，改善患者预后。

A clotbusting drug commonly used to treat ischemic stroke interacts negatively with a promising anti-inflammatory treatment (anakinra), underscoring the need to test new stroke therapies alongside existing standard care. According to The University of Manchester–led study on mice, published in Stroke, the timing of anakinra must be adjusted to avoid reducing the benefits of the clot-busting therapy known as tissue plasminogen activator (tPA).

Stroke is the second leading cause of death and disability worldwide; experts estimate the number of people affected could rise by more than 80% over the next 25 years. But despite decades of research and thousands of experimental drugs, the only approved medicines for treating the most common type of stroke—ischemic stroke—are clot-busting drugs known as plasminogen activators, like tPA.

Though tPA can be lifesaving for acute ischemic stroke, about 2%–6% of treated patients develop potentially fatal brain bleeding, according to the ECASS III trial of the early 2000s. It must be given within 4.5 hours of symptom onset, but many patients arrive too late or don't know when their symptoms started.

Scientists now know that inflammation plays a major role in worsening brain injury after a stroke, mostly driven by a molecule called interleukin-1 (IL-1). Anakinra—an interleukin-1 receptor antagonist (IL-1Ra)—blocks IL-1 and has shown promise in reducing inflammation in both laboratory and early clinical studies of stroke.

However, a phase II clinical trial known as SCIL-STROKE based at The Northern Care Alliance NHS Foundation Trust found that IL-1Ra did not improve patient recovery overall. "The findings of SCIL-STROKE raise questions about whether the drug might interact negatively with standard clot-busting treatment," said lead author Dr. Ioana-Emilia Mosneag, based at the University of Manchester.

Because nearly three-quarters of patients in the SCIL-STROKE trial received the clot-busting drug tPA before IL-1Ra, the researchers set out to investigate whether the two treatments might negatively interact with each other.

They re-examined data from the SCIL-STROKE trial and discovered that patients who received tPA before IL-1Ra had significantly lower levels of IL-1Ra in their blood, suggesting the drug was being broken down. Laboratory research confirmed that IL-1Ra can be cut apart by plasmin, an enzyme produced during tPA treatment, meaning the anti-inflammatory drug may be degraded before it can work. Researchers then tested the interaction in a mouse model of stroke, using dosing schedules that matched those used in the clinical trial.

When IL-1Ra was given after tPA, no harmful interaction was seen, and the protective effects of tPA were preserved. However, when IL-1Ra was given at the same time as tPA—during the clot-busting process—the benefits of tPA were dramatically reduced, with brain damage shrinking by only 15% compared to 68% with tPA alone.

The mice receiving both drugs together also showed poorer blood flow in the brain, more inflammatory immune cells entering damaged tissue, and higher levels of harmful structures called neutrophil extracellular traps. This indicates that the drug interaction is also detrimental to the anti-inflammatory effect of IL-1Ra.

Dr. Mosneag added, "Our findings suggest that IL-1Ra can interfere with tPA's ability to dissolve clots when the two drugs are present in the bloodstream at the same time. The results also help explain why IL-1Ra levels were lower in patients who received tPA first, as plasmin generated during clot-busting appears to break down IL-1Ra.

"However, the effect of tPA on IL-RA—the opposite order—isn't necessarily a problem as IL-1RA was still active in reducing IL-6 in the SCIL-STROKE study, but this needs further evaluation."

Co-author Professor Stuart Allan from The University of Manchester said, "This study shows that timing is very likely to be a critical factor in the efficacy of IL-1Ra, which will be beneficial if given after tPA rather than alongside it. We also need to test whether similar interactions occur with other clot-busting drugs such as tenecteplase, which may be less likely to break down IL-1Ra due to its greater specificity."

Co-author Professor Craig Smith from the University of Manchester said, "This study has important implications for further development of IL-1Ra as a treatment for ischemic stroke, where there remains a focus on maximizing delivery of thrombolysis drugs to eligible patients as quickly as possible in clinical care. Future studies will need to investigate the timing and effectiveness of IL-1Ra treatment after receiving tPA."

一种常用于治疗缺血性卒中的溶栓药物与一种有前景的抗炎疗法（阿那白滞素）存在负面相互作用，这凸显了在现有标准治疗方案之外，测试新型卒中疗法的必要性。根据《卒中》杂志发表的曼彻斯特大学主导的小鼠研究，必须调整阿那白滞素的给药时间，以避免降低组织型纤溶酶原激活剂（tPA）这种溶栓疗法的益处。

卒中是全球第二大死因和致残原因；专家估计，未来25年内受影响人数可能增加80%以上。然而，尽管经过数十年的研究和数千种实验药物的尝试，目前唯一获批用于治疗最常见类型卒中（缺血性卒中）的药物仍是纤溶酶原激活剂类溶栓药物，例如tPA。

虽然tPA对急性缺血性卒中患者可能具有挽救生命的作用，但根据21世纪初的ECASS III试验，约有2%-6%的接受治疗患者会出现可能致命的脑出血。该药必须在症状发作后4.5小时内给药，但许多患者就诊过晚，或者不清楚症状何时开始。

科学家们现在认识到，炎症在卒中后脑损伤恶化中扮演着重要角色，主要由一种名为白细胞介素-1（IL-1）的分子驱动。阿那白滞素——一种白细胞介素-1受体拮抗剂（IL-1Ra）——能够阻断IL-1，并在卒中的实验室和早期临床研究中显示出减少炎症的希望。

然而，一项由北护理联盟NHS基金会信托牵头的名为SCIL-STROKE的II期临床试验发现，IL-1Ra并未全面改善患者康复。“SCIL-STROKE的发现引发了关于该药物是否可能与标准溶栓治疗产生负面相互作用的疑问，”曼彻斯特大学的首席作者Ioana-Emilia Mosneag博士表示。

由于SCIL-STROKE试验中近四分之三的患者在接受IL-1Ra之前接受了溶栓药物tPA，研究人员着手调查这两种治疗是否可能产生负面相互作用。

他们重新审查了SCIL-STROKE试验的数据，发现那些在接受IL-1Ra之前接受tPA的患者，其血液中IL-1Ra的水平显著降低，这表明该药物正在被分解。实验室研究证实，IL-1Ra可被纤溶酶（一种在tPA治疗过程中产生的酶）切割分解，这意味着这种抗炎药物可能在发挥作用之前就被降解了。随后，研究人员在卒中小鼠模型中测试了这种相互作用，采用了与临床试验中相匹配的给药方案。

当IL-1Ra在tPA之后给药时，未观察到有害相互作用，且tPA的保护作用得以保留。然而，当IL-1Ra与tPA同时给药时——即在溶栓过程中——tPA的益处显著降低，脑损伤仅缩小了15%，而单独使用tPA时则缩小了68%。

同时接受两种药物的小鼠还表现出大脑血流不良，更多炎症性免疫细胞进入受损组织，以及更高水平的有害结构，称为中性粒细胞胞外陷阱。这表明药物相互作用对IL-1Ra的抗炎作用也是不利的。

Mosneag博士补充说：“我们的发现表明，当IL-1Ra和tPA两种药物同时存在于血液中时，IL-1Ra会干扰tPA溶解血栓的能力。这些结果还有助于解释为什么先接受tPA的患者IL-1Ra水平较低，因为溶栓过程中产生的纤溶酶似乎会分解IL-1Ra。”

“然而，tPA对IL-1Ra的影响——即相反的给药顺序——不一定是问题，因为在SCIL-STROKE研究中，IL-1Ra在降低IL-6方面仍然活跃，但这需要进一步评估。”

曼彻斯特大学的共同作者Stuart Allan教授表示：“这项研究表明，给药时间很可能是IL-1Ra疗效的关键因素，如果是在tPA之后而不是同时给药，将会更有益。我们还需要测试是否与其他溶栓药物（如替奈普酶）发生类似的相互作用，替奈普酶由于其更高的特异性，可能不太可能分解IL-1Ra。”

曼彻斯特大学的共同作者Craig Smith教授表示：“这项研究对IL-1Ra作为缺血性卒中治疗的进一步开发具有重要意义，临床护理中仍然关注尽可能快地将溶栓药物送达符合条件的患者。未来的研究将需要调查在接受tPA后IL-1Ra治疗的时间和有效性。”
