Location, location, location: For potassium channels, it depends on functionality

Coupling of KCNQ2/3 functionality to localization. Researchers demonstrate that while normal KCNQ2/3 (left) is strongly enriched at the AIS, dysfunctional KCNQ2/3 (right) shows a significant reduction in AIS localization efficiency. The arrow indicates the position of AIS. Scale bar, 20 µm. Credit: Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (2026). DOI: 10.1073/pnas.2527749123

Potassium KCNQ2/3 channels are crucial for suppressing the excitability of brain cells, or neurons. When these channels don't work properly, they can cause specific types of epilepsy like benign familial neonatal convulsions and early infantile epileptic encephalopathy. In "Coupling of Functionality to Trafficking of KCNQ2/3 Potassium Channels at the Axon Initial Segment," published in PNAS, Japanese researchers reveal the relationship between KCNQ2/3 channel functionality (i.e., how well they work to control electrical signals in neurons) and localization (i.e., where they are found inside a cell), with important implications for the treatment of these epileptic disorders.

For KCNQ2/3 channels to work properly in the brain, they must have full functionality and be located in the correct cellular region—specifically the axon initial segment (AIS), the site in neurons where electric signals are first triggered, controlling nerve cell activity. This led the research team to wonder: does the functionality of KCNQ2/3 channels affect their cellular localization, or are the two not linked at all?

To investigate this potential association, the research team first genetically engineered the functionality of the channels, and then used channel trafficking imaging to visualize whether the channels were taken to their location in the AIS.

In this way, they demonstrated that KCNQ2/3 functionality was indeed linked to its trafficking to the correct cellular localization. What's more, when they used single-molecule imaging, they could see that reduced KCNQ3 functionality actually reduced the AIS localization of KCNQ2/3 by altering the entire trafficking pathway.

Binding kinetics of KCNQ2/3 to ankG depend on channel functionality. KCNQ2/3 with normal functionality binds more stably to ankG. This promotes exocytic insertion of KCNQ2/3 at the AIS surface while suppressing its endocytic internalization. In contrast, reduced KCNQ2/3 functionality impairs its binding to ankG, leading to decreased insertion and enhanced internalization at the AIS surface. Credit: Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (2026). DOI: 10.1073/pnas.2527749123

"Because we already knew that the localization of KCNQ2/3 to the AIS is regulated by a protein known as ankyrinG, or ankG, we next decided to explore the interactions between full-length KCNQ3 and ankG," explains lead author of the study Daisuke Yoshioka.

"We found that the active conformation of KCNQ3 was essential for its stable binding to ankG, further confirming that functional KCNQ2/3 is needed to ensure its proper accumulation at the AIS."

Together, these findings highlight the mechanisms underlying the important link between KCNQ2/3 functionality and localization, and provide clues about how their alterations might affect neuronal excitability.

"Now that we know that KCNQ2/3 functionality is closely linked to its localization at the AIS, we have a more concrete target for studying disorders involving altered KCNQ2/3 channels," says Yasushi Okamura, senior author of the study. "Keeping these potassium channels functioning properly may also be important for ensuring they reach the proper location in brain cells."

Given that KCNQ2/3‑related neurological disorders, including epilepsy, remain relatively poorly understood and can be difficult to treat, these findings have important implications. They may contribute to the development of new therapeutic strategies that improve the quality of life for young patients and their families.

钾离子KCNQ2/3通道对于抑制脑细胞（即神经元）的兴奋性至关重要。当这些通道功能异常时，可能导致特定类型的癫痫，如良性家族性新生儿惊厥和早期婴儿癫痫性脑病。在日本研究人员发表于《美国国家科学院院刊》（PNAS）的论文“KCNQ2/3钾离子通道在轴突始段的功能与转运偶联”中，他们揭示了KCNQ2/3通道功能（即其控制神经元电信号的有效性）与定位（即其在细胞内的存在位置）之间的关系，这对于这些癫痫性疾病的治疗具有重要意义。

为了使KCNQ2/3通道在大脑中正常发挥作用，它们必须具有完整的功能性，并定位于正确的细胞区域——特别是轴突始段（AIS），这是神经元中电信号首次被触发并控制神经细胞活动的部位。这促使研究团队思考：KCNQ2/3通道的功能性是否会影响其细胞定位，或者两者之间根本没有关联？

为了探究这种潜在关联，研究团队首先通过基因工程改造了通道的功能性，然后利用通道转运成像技术来观察这些通道是否被运送到轴突始段的特定位置。

通过这种方式，他们证实了KCNQ2/3的功能性确实与其转运至正确的细胞定位相关联。此外，当他们使用单分子成像技术时，他们发现KCNQ3功能减弱实际上通过改变整个转运途径而降低了KCNQ2/3在轴突始段的定位。

KCNQ2/3与ankG的结合动力学取决于通道的功能性。具有正常功能的KCNQ2/3与ankG结合更稳定。这促进了KCNQ2/3在AIS表面的胞吐性插入，同时抑制了其胞吞性内化。相反，KCNQ2/3功能减弱会损害其与ankG的结合，导致AIS表面插入减少和内化增强。图片来源：《美国国家科学院院刊》（2026）。DOI: 10.1073/pnas.2527749123

“因为我们已知KCNQ2/3在轴突始段的定位是由一种名为锚蛋白G（ankyrinG，简称ankG）的蛋白质调控的，所以我们接下来决定探究全长KCNQ3与ankG之间的相互作用，”该研究的主要作者Daisuke Yoshioka解释道。

“我们发现KCNQ3的活性构象对其与ankG的稳定结合至关重要，这进一步证实了功能性KCNQ2/3是确保其在轴突始段正确积累所必需的。”

综上所述，这些发现阐明了KCNQ2/3功能性与定位之间重要联系的潜在机制，并为它们的改变如何影响神经元兴奋性提供了线索。

“现在我们知道了KCNQ2/3的功能性与其在轴突始段的定位紧密相关，这为研究涉及KCNQ2/3通道改变的疾病提供了更具体的目标，”该研究的资深作者Yasushi Okamura表示。“确保这些钾离子通道正常运作，对于它们能够到达脑细胞中的正确位置也可能至关重要。”

鉴于KCNQ2/3相关神经系统疾病，包括癫痫，目前仍相对了解不足且难以治疗，这些发现具有重要意义。它们可能有助于开发新的治疗策略，从而改善年轻患者及其家庭的生活质量。
