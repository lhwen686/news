Original Text
On Dec 8, 2025, we were informed of a serious conflict of interest in a Lancet publication. We thank David Rosner and Gerald Markowitz for their rigorous investigation in drawing attention to an unsigned commentary published in 1977 in The Lancet,1 which concluded that, “there is no reason to believe that normal consumer exposure to cosmetic talc has in the past led either to cancer at any site or to measurable loss of lung function. It seems unlikely that future exposure to cosmetic talc of the specifications now agreed to by major manufacturers will present a health hazard”.
