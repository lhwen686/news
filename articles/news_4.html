中风后失语症如何阻碍流利言语

How post-stroke aphasia disrupts fluent speech

💡 AI 深度解析 (AI Insight)

本研究深入探讨了中风后失语症的神经机制，特别是言语感觉-运动整合功能障碍。核心概念在于大脑如何利用“听觉反馈”来监测和纠正言语输出。正常情况下，大脑会预测即将发出的言语，并与实际听到的自身言语进行比较。若存在差异（即“言语错误信号”），感觉系统会触发纠正性运动指令，调整构音肌肉，确保言语流畅准确。研究采用的“听觉反馈扰动”技术巧妙地模拟了这种错误信号，通过实时改变患者自身言语并反馈，从而精确探测了患者大脑识别和处理言语错误的能力。结果显示，失语症患者的“事件相关电位（ERP）”活动显著减弱，表明其神经系统在处理这些错误信号时存在缺陷。这一发现超越了传统上仅关注言语运动输出的治疗范式，提示未来治疗应更注重恢复听觉反馈处理和感觉-运动整合功能。这可能促使开发出靶向神经可塑性训练或利用脑机接口技术的新型干预措施，以重建或增强失语症患者的言语自我监测和纠正能力，从而显著提升康复效果和生活质量。

A study led by a speech neuroscientist at The University of Texas at Dallas sheds light on how damage from stroke disrupts the brain mechanisms required for fluent speech. The research, published in NeuroImage, could help advance future treatment approaches for individuals with post-stroke aphasia.

How the brain monitors speech

Led by Dr. Roozbeh Behroozmand, associate professor of speech, language, and hearing in the School of Behavioral and Brain Sciences, the researchers recorded brain signals in stroke survivors with aphasia to understand how lesions in neural networks of the left hemisphere interfere with the patients' ability to process auditory feedback—a critical component in producing accurate, fluent speech.

"The auditory system is essential in identifying errors as we speak," said Behroozmand, the corresponding author of the study. "We control our speech production based on what we're hearing from our own verbal output to maintain fluent communication."

Aphasia affects 20% to 40% of stroke survivors, impairing speech production, comprehension, reading and writing without diminishing a person's intellectual ability.

"Clinical symptoms of aphasia are diverse, ranging from nonfluent speech to difficulty understanding spoken language," Behroozmand said. "Some individuals can comprehend speech but not produce it; some lose words for naming objects; some have issues with repeating words. These outcomes all represent errors in the sensory and motor systems of the brain for monitoring, detecting errors and generating corrective motor commands to the speech muscles—the physical movements that will produce words and sentences."

Limits of current aphasia therapies

Clinical intervention for post-stroke aphasia patients has traditionally focused on improving speech movement outcomes. Behroozmand explained that such treatment only addresses one of three major components of the brain's effort to correct speech errors.

"Detecting the error is the first step: Your brain anticipates what you intend to say before you overtly produce words, and then you hear your own feedback to determine if the right words were produced," he said. "If that is not the case, the second step is to process the errors you hear and translate them into corrective motor commands that adjust muscle movements to reach speech targets. The last step is execution of those physical modifications to ensure corrected words are produced.

"Our findings underscore the importance of the auditory feedback processing step in identifying and correcting speech errors."

Experiment reveals disrupted error signals

Researchers studied sensory-motor integration in speech production using auditory feedback perturbation, which is a technique that externally alters participants' own speech and feeds the manipulated version back to them via headphones in real time.

"Using this technique, we artificially generate a speech error signal for the brain, which is interpreted as a disparity between intended and actual speech output when patients are talking," Behroozmand said. "Then, we measure how this mismatch leads to an adjustment in speech output to probe the integrity of sensory and motor systems in healthy individuals and identify the source of neural deficits in patients with post-stroke aphasia.

"In our study, we found that participants in the aphasia group showed significantly reduced event-related potential activity—an indicator of weakened neural responses—compared with the neurotypical speakers. These patterns point to disruptions across the sensory-motor brain networks responsible for detecting and correcting speech errors."

The study confirmed that post-stroke aphasia individuals have a diminished ability to recognize their own speech errors in the auditory feedback and to make real-time adjustments to maintain fluency.

Next steps for brain-based research

In further research, investigators in Behroozmand's Speech Neuroscience Lab will use electroencephalograms and neuroimaging from different parts of the brain to understand better how stroke affects the interaction between the sensory and motor systems during speech production.

"It could be that both the hearing system and the motor system are intact, but the integration between the two breaks down," Behroozmand said. "Stroke patients with aphasia could exhibit failures at any of these levels, depending on the location and size of the lesion in the brain and how much gray matter or white matter tracts have been damaged."

德克萨斯大学达拉斯分校的一位言语神经科学家领导的一项研究，揭示了中风造成的损伤如何扰乱流利言语所需的脑机制。这项发表在《神经影像学》杂志上的研究，有望推动未来中风后失语症患者的治疗方法。

大脑如何监测言语

在行为与脑科学学院言语、语言和听力学副教授Roozbeh Behroozmand博士的带领下，研究人员记录了失语症中风幸存者的大脑信号，以了解左半球神经网络中的病灶如何干扰患者处理听觉反馈的能力——这是产生准确、流畅言语的关键组成部分。

“听觉系统对于我们在说话时识别错误至关重要，”该研究的通讯作者Behroozmand说。“我们根据自己口头输出所听到的反馈来控制言语产生，以维持流畅的交流。”

失语症影响20%到40%的中风幸存者，它会损害言语产生、理解、阅读和写作能力，但不会降低一个人的智力。

“失语症的临床症状多种多样，从言语不流利到理解口语困难，”Behroozmand说。“有些人能理解言语但无法产生言语；有些人则在命名物体时找不到词语；还有些人在重复词语时遇到问题。这些结果都代表了大脑感觉和运动系统在监测、检测错误和向言语肌肉生成纠正性运动指令（即产生词语和句子的物理动作）方面出现了错误。”

当前失语症疗法的局限性

中风后失语症患者的临床干预传统上侧重于改善言语运动结果。Behroozmand解释说，这种治疗只解决了大脑纠正言语错误三大主要组成部分之一。

“检测错误是第一步：在你明确说出词语之前，你的大脑会预测你打算说什么，然后你会听到自己的反馈，以确定是否发出了正确的词语，”他说。“如果不是，第二步是处理你听到的错误，并将其转化为纠正性运动指令，以调整肌肉运动来达到言语目标。最后一步是执行这些物理修正，以确保产生纠正后的词语。”

“我们的研究结果强调了听觉反馈处理步骤在识别和纠正言语错误中的重要性。”

实验揭示错误信号中断

研究人员使用听觉反馈扰动技术研究了言语产生中的感觉-运动整合。该技术通过外部方式实时改变参与者自己的言语，并通过耳机将其操纵后的版本反馈给他们。

Behroozmand说：“使用这项技术，我们人为地为大脑生成一个言语错误信号，当患者说话时，这被解释为意图言语和实际言语输出之间的差异。”“然后，我们测量这种不匹配如何导致言语输出的调整，以探测健康个体感觉和运动系统的完整性，并识别中风后失语症患者神经缺陷的来源。”

“在我们的研究中，我们发现失语症组的参与者表现出显著降低的事件相关电位活动——这是神经反应减弱的指标——相比之下，神经典型个体则没有。这些模式表明负责检测和纠正言语错误的感觉-运动脑网络发生了中断。”

这项研究证实，中风后失语症患者在听觉反馈中识别自身言语错误和进行实时调整以保持流畅性的能力有所下降。

脑研究的下一步

在进一步的研究中，Behroozmand的言语神经科学实验室的调查人员将使用脑电图和来自大脑不同区域的神经影像学技术，以更好地理解中风在言语产生过程中如何影响感觉和运动系统之间的相互作用。

Behroozmand说：“可能是听觉系统和运动系统都完好无损，但两者之间的整合出现了问题。”“失语症中风患者可能在这些层面中的任何一个表现出功能障碍，这取决于大脑病灶的位置和大小，以及有多少灰质或白质束受到损伤。”
