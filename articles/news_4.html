构建具有增强药物代谢酶活性的人肝细胞模型

Developing a human hepatocyte model with enhanced drug-metabolizing enzyme activity

Establishment and characterization of FLAG-CYP2D6 and CYP2D6-iGFP undifferentiated HepaRG cells. Credit: Dr. Shinpei Yamaguchi

A research team led by Associate Professor Shinpei Yamaguchi and the late Professor Masako Tada of the Faculty of Science, together with Professor Yojiro Anzai and Lecturer Yohei Iizaka of the Faculty of Pharmaceutical Sciences at Toho University, have developed a new HepaRG cell line with markedly enhanced activity of CYP2D6, a major drug-metabolizing enzyme. This engineered human hepatocyte model enables more human-relevant evaluation of drug-induced hepatotoxicity mediated by CYP2D6. The study was published in the journal PLOS ONE on December 29, 2025.

The researchers established a next-generation hepatocyte model with metabolic capacity comparable to adult human liver tissue. By enhancing CYP2D6 metabolic activity to approximately 8,000-fold relative to conventional HepaRG cells, the team successfully reproduced expression levels comparable to adult human liver tissue in vitro.

Their robust model demonstrated practical utility, achieved through green fluorescent protein (GFP) visualization and stable functional maintenance. GFP co-expression enabled real-time monitoring of CYP2D6 expression, and high expression levels were maintained even after differentiation into hepatocyte-like cells, allowing detailed tracking of complex drug responses.

The findings have implications for early prediction of drug toxicity and an alternative to animal testing. Experiments with perhexiline, a drug primarily metabolized by CYP2D6, demonstrated improved cell viability in the CYP2D6-enhanced HepaRG cell model. This system facilitates prediction of toxicity associated with inter-individual variability in human CYP2D6 activity, which is difficult to reproduce in animal models, thereby supporting early-stage drug safety assessments and serving as a promising in vitro alternative to animal testing.

由东邦大学理学部副教授山口真平及已故多田真砂子教授，以及药学部教授安西洋次郎及讲师饭坂洋平共同领导的研究团队，开发出一种新型HepaRG细胞系，其CYP2D6（一种主要的药物代谢酶）活性显著增强。这种工程化的人肝细胞模型能够对CYP2D6介导的药物性肝毒性进行更具人体相关性的评估。该研究于2025年12月29日发表在《PLOS ONE》杂志上。

研究人员建立了一种具有与成人肝脏组织相当的代谢能力的新一代肝细胞模型。通过将CYP2D6代谢活性相对于传统HepaRG细胞提高了约8,000倍，该团队成功地在体外再现了与成人肝脏组织相当的表达水平。

他们强大的模型展示了实用性，通过绿色荧光蛋白（GFP）可视化和稳定的功能维持来实现。GFP的共表达使得CYP2D6表达的实时监测成为可能，并且即使在分化为肝细胞样细胞后，高表达水平仍能维持，从而实现对复杂药物反应的详细追踪。

这些发现对药物毒性的早期预测以及动物试验替代方案具有重要意义。使用主要由CYP2D6代谢的药物哌西林进行的实验，在CYP2D6增强型HepaRG细胞模型中显示出改善的细胞活力。该系统有助于预测与人类CYP2D6活性个体间差异相关的毒性，而这种差异在动物模型中难以重现，从而支持早期药物安全性评估，并作为一种有前景的体外动物试验替代方案。
