研究指出难治性肺癌的潜在新疗法

Study points to potential new therapies for hard-to-treat lung cancers

💡 AI 深度解析 (AI Insight)

本研究深入探讨了非小细胞肺癌（NSCLC）对免疫疗法无反应后的治疗困境，并揭示了一种新的抗肿瘤机制及联合治疗策略。核心机制在于，肿瘤细胞在受到溶酶体抑制时，会通过激活固醇调节元件结合蛋白-1（SREBP-1）来增加葡萄糖摄取，从而形成一个葡萄糖-脂质代谢的正向调控反馈环，以实现代谢代偿并抵抗治疗。溶酶体作为细胞内的“回收站”，在肿瘤细胞的生长和生存中扮演关键角色。然而，单一抑制溶酶体（如使用氯喹）往往效果不佳，正是因为肿瘤细胞能够通过SREBP-1介导的葡萄糖代谢途径进行代偿。本研究的突破在于提出，同时靶向溶酶体功能和SREBP-1介导的葡萄糖-脂质代谢反馈环，能够诱导线粒体损伤和氧化应激，最终导致肿瘤细胞死亡。这一“双重打击”策略不仅为缺乏可操作驱动基因突变的肺鳞癌和腺癌患者提供了新的治疗希望，而且通过将已获批的“老药新用”药物（如氯喹和辛伐他汀）与处于临床试验阶段的抑制剂结合，极大地加速了临床转化的可行性。其更广泛的行业影响在于，这种代谢双重靶向的思路可能适用于其他代谢需求旺盛的肿瘤，为克服肿瘤耐药性提供了普适性方法论。

Findings from a study led by researchers at The Ohio State University Comprehensive Cancer Center–Arthur G. James Cancer Hospital and Richard J. Solove Research Institute (OSUCCC–James) support the potential of new therapies that could improve clinical outcomes for patients with squamous and adenocarcinoma non-small cell lung cancers (NSCLC) that don't respond to immunotherapy. The study is published in the journal Science Translational Medicine.

Dual-targeting a tumor survival pathway

The researchers say their study provides strong evidence for the possible effectiveness of therapies that target both lysosomes (particles in cells that help maintain cellular stability and nutrient availability) and a protein called SREBP-1 that increases glucose uptake and helps tumors resist current therapies that inhibit lysosomes.

Corresponding and senior author for the study was Deliang Guo, Ph.D., founding director of the Center for Cancer Metabolism at the OSUCCC–James. Yaogang Zhong, Ph.D., a senior researcher in Guo's lab, was the first author.

"These findings reveal a previously unrecognized mechanism by which tumors withstand lysosomal inhibition, providing a strong rationale for combination strategies targeting lysosomal function alongside glucose and lipid metabolism to more effectively treat NSCLC," said Guo. "This approach may also be applicable to other cancers with high metabolic demands for glucose and lipids that would present an even broader strategy for enhancing therapeutic outcomes."

Why lysosome inhibition falls short

The scientists say that functional lysosomes are critical for tumor growth, but multiple efforts to inhibit lysosomes in several cancer types with a drug called chloroquine (CQ) in combination with radiation, chemotherapy, and targeted agents have yielded only modest or partial responses in clinical trials.

This preclinical study, which involved cell lines and animal models, sought to learn how tumor cells evade lysosomal suppression, and also identify strategies for overcoming this resistance.

Researchers also demonstrated that inhibiting glucose transport overcomes tumor resistance to CQ treatment by inducing mitochondrial damage, oxidative stress, and tumor cell death.

A feedback loop and new drug combos

"Our study is the first to reveal a previously unrecognized mode in which glucose and lipid metabolism are coupled to form a positive regulatory feedback loop," said Guo. "This finding deepens our understanding of the regulation of complex metabolic networks in biological systems and uncovers the metabolic compensatory flexibility of tumors, as well as their ability to evade inhibition of a single metabolic node."

"This study provides clear mechanistic guidance and a feasible drug-combination strategy to markedly enhance the antitumor efficacy of lysosomal inhibitors," said Zhong, adding that this work proposes that simultaneous targeting of lysosomal function and the glucose-lipid metabolic positive feedback loop represents an efficient antitumor strategy.

"This approach is particularly suitable for patients with lung squamous cell carcinoma and subsets of lung adenocarcinoma who lack actionable driver mutations and have limited treatment options," said Zhong.

Notably, CQ and simvastatin are both clinically approved, repurposed drugs. The fatty acid synthesis inhibitor TVB-2640 has already entered phase II/III clinical trials, greatly accelerating the feasibility of clinical translation and the validation of this combination therapy strategy.

双重靶向肿瘤生存通路

研究人员表示，他们的研究为同时靶向溶酶体（细胞内有助于维持细胞稳定性和营养物质可用性的细胞器）和一种名为SREBP-1的蛋白质的疗法提供了强有力的证据，SREBP-1会增加葡萄糖摄取并帮助肿瘤抵抗当前抑制溶酶体的疗法。

该研究的通讯作者和资深作者是OSUCCC–James癌症代谢中心创始主任Deliang Guo博士。Guo实验室的高级研究员Yaogang Zhong博士是第一作者。

Guo博士表示：“这些发现揭示了肿瘤抵抗溶酶体抑制的一种此前未被认识的机制，为结合靶向溶酶体功能以及葡萄糖和脂质代谢的策略提供了强有力的理论依据，以更有效地治疗非小细胞肺癌。这种方法也可能适用于对葡萄糖和脂质有高代谢需求的其他癌症，从而提供更广泛的策略来增强治疗效果。”

为何溶酶体抑制效果不佳

科学家们表示，功能性溶酶体对肿瘤生长至关重要，但在多项研究中，尝试使用氯喹（CQ）与放射疗法、化学疗法和靶向药物联合抑制多种癌症类型的溶酶体，在临床试验中仅取得了适度或部分缓解。

这项涉及细胞系和动物模型的临床前研究旨在了解肿瘤细胞如何逃避溶酶体抑制，并确定克服这种抵抗的策略。

研究人员还证明，抑制葡萄糖转运通过诱导线粒体损伤、氧化应激和肿瘤细胞死亡，克服了肿瘤对CQ治疗的抵抗。

一个反馈环和新的药物组合

Guo博士说：“我们的研究首次揭示了葡萄糖和脂质代谢如何偶联形成正向调控反馈环的先前未被认识的模式。这一发现加深了我们对生物系统中复杂代谢网络调控的理解，并揭示了肿瘤的代谢代偿灵活性，以及它们逃避单一代谢节点抑制的能力。”

Zhong博士补充说：“这项研究提供了明确的机制指导和可行的药物组合策略，以显著增强溶酶体抑制剂的抗肿瘤疗效。”他指出，这项工作提出同时靶向溶酶体功能和葡萄糖-脂质代谢正反馈环代表一种有效的抗肿瘤策略。

Zhong博士表示：“这种方法特别适用于缺乏可操作的驱动基因突变且治疗选择有限的肺鳞状细胞癌和部分肺腺癌患者。”

值得注意的是，氯喹和辛伐他汀都是临床批准的、老药新用的药物。脂肪酸合成抑制剂TVB-2640已进入II/III期临床试验，这极大地加快了临床转化和这种联合治疗策略验证的可行性。
