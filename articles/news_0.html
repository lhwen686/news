Original Text
Definitive chemoradiotherapy remains the cornerstone of treatment for locally advanced oropharyngeal squamous-cell carcinoma. The transition from 3D conformal radiotherapy to intensity-modulated radiotherapy (IMRT) fundamentally transformed toxicity outcomes, reducing xerostomia and long-term swallowing dysfunction in landmark head and neck trials.1–4 These issues have become increasingly relevant as the epidemiology of oropharyngeal squamous-cell carcinoma has shifted towards human papillomavirus-associated disease in younger patients with long life expectancy.
