Original Text
The March, 2026, decision by the French drug regulatory agency to approve intravenous racemic ketamine for the treatment of adult severe suicidal crisis1 represents a major step forward in the management of suicidal risk. This national authorisation in France, the first of its kind worldwide, recognises not only ketamine's clinical potential, but also the absence of any officially approved, evidence-based, rapid-acting pharmacological alternative when suicide risk is imminent.
