研究发现免疫细胞和RANK蛋白是生育调节因子

Study identifies immune cells and RANK protein as fertility regulators

这项研究深入揭示了下丘脑-垂体-性腺（HPG）轴——调控青春期、性腺成熟及生育能力的核心内分泌轴——中，小胶质细胞和RANK蛋白扮演的关键且此前未被识别的角色。传统上认为HPG轴主要由神经元调控，但此发现表明，中枢神经系统的固有免疫细胞小胶质细胞，通过表达RANK蛋白，直接调节促性腺激素释放神经元（GnRH神经元）的功能。动物模型中，RANK蛋白缺失或抑制导致性激素水平降低、性腺功能减退和不育；人类患者中也发现编码RANK蛋白基因的突变与先天性促性腺激素缺乏性性腺功能减退症相关。

此研究具有显著的医学和行业影响。它颠覆了对生育能力神经内分泌调控的传统认知，将免疫细胞纳入生殖轴调控网络。RANK蛋白因此成为治疗多种内分泌失调及不育症的潜在新型治疗靶点，并可作为相关疾病分子诊断的候选基因。该发现还提示，小胶质细胞-RANK调节机制可能广泛应用于其他重要神经内分泌轴（如食欲-饱腹轴、应激轴），为未来代谢和神经精神疾病研究开辟新方向。这项跨学科研究也再次证明了多领域合作在攻克复杂生物医学难题中的重要性。
Original Text
The kick off signal for puberty begins in the brain. Specifically, in the hypothalamus, where specific neurons release a hormone that activates the hypophysis, at the base of the skull, which then releases other hormones to start gonad –ovaries or testicles –maturation. This mechanism leading to a fertile organism is the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis.

A study by Spain's National Cancer Research Centre (CNIO) has just discovered in animal models that two previously unsuspected elements are also involved in this hormone regulating system: microglia – defensive cells of the nervous system – and the protein RANK, which contributes to bone remodelling and is essential in the functioning of the mammary glands.

The article is published in the journal Science. It is led by Eva González-Suárez, head of the CNIO Transformation and Metastasis Group, who discovered in 2010 the key role played by RANK in the development of breast cancer. The first author is Alejandro Collado, a researcher from the same group and co- corresponding author.

Immune cells to modulate fertility

The hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis regulates many processes related to reproduction. Its main players in the hypothalamus are gonadotropin-releasing neurons (GnRH). Gonadotropins are two hormones that control the onset of puberty, the development of the gonads, and fertility. It was previously known that GnRH neurons are modulated by other neurons, but not that immune cells could influence their functioning.

This is the newly discovered function of microglia, cells in the central nervous system that eliminate potential threats and molecules that serve no purpose.

Finding fertility-regulating cells that are not neurons, but rather immune cells, is important." Eva González-Suárez, head of the CNIO Transformation and Metastasis Group

The study shows that the way microglia regulate the function of GnRH neurons is by expressing the RANK protein.

When the CNIO group suppressed RANK expression in animal models, the reproductive function became distorted, both in males and females. In specimens born without RANK, or when it was removed in prepubescent animals, there was a reduction in sex hormones and a loss of gonad functionality known as hypogonadism, and puberty did not occur in these animals. When RANK was eliminated in sexually mature specimens, they became infertile within a month.

New mutations for a human syndrome

To investigate whether RANK might play a role in human fertility, researchers genetically analysed samples from patients with congenital hypogonadotropic hypogonadism, a rare syndrome associated with delayed or absent puberty and infertility. It was known that it is caused by problems in GnRH neurons or in the molecules they produce. The research identified mutations in the gene encoding the RANK protein in some patients.

"These results show that RANK could be a therapeutic target for endocrine disorders and syndromes affecting fertility, as well as a candidate gene for the molecular diagnosis of congenital hypogonadotropic hypogonadism," according to the authors.

González-Suárez emphasizes that "the role of microglia in regulating the function of 'reproductive' neurons is new, and this regulation associated with RANK can occur in other axes, for other functions, such as the appetite-satiety axis, the stress axis, etc."

The importance of collaboration

The authors also want to highlight this research as an example of the importance of interdisciplinary collaboration. "My doctoral thesis started out with the question of whether the RANK protein played any role in the development of mammary tissue, in the breast itself, during puberty," Collado explains. "When we realised that we needed to explore issues implying fertility, neurons and brain cells, we started consulting colleagues from other fields."

Thus the team began collaborating with Manuel Tena-Sempere, from the University of Córdoba and the Maimónides Biomedical Research Institute in Córdoba (IMIBIC), Vincent Prevot from Inserm (France's National Institute of Health and Medical Research), Rafael Fernández Chacón from the Biomedicine Institute (IBiS) in Seville, and Nelly Pitteleoud from the Centre Hospitalier Universitaire Vaudois (CHUV) in Switzerland.

"We have reached conclusions that we could not have foreseen and learned techniques and tools that we will now be able to apply to future studies," Collado states.

青春期的启动信号始于大脑。具体来说，是在下丘脑，特定的神经元释放一种激素，激活位于颅底的垂体，垂体随后释放其他激素以启动性腺（卵巢或睾丸）的成熟。这种导致生育能力形成的机制即为下丘脑-垂体-性腺（HPG）轴。

西班牙国家癌症研究中心（CNIO）的一项研究刚刚在动物模型中发现，两个此前未曾怀疑的元素也参与了这一激素调节系统：小胶质细胞（神经系统的防御细胞）和RANK蛋白，后者有助于骨重塑并对乳腺功能至关重要。

该文章发表在《科学》杂志上。研究由CNIO转化与转移小组负责人Eva González-Suárez领导，她于2010年发现了RANK在乳腺癌发展中的关键作用。第一作者是该小组的研究员兼共同通讯作者Alejandro Collado。

免疫细胞调节生育能力
下丘脑-垂体-性腺轴调节着许多与生殖相关的过程。其在下丘脑中的主要参与者是促性腺激素释放神经元（GnRH神经元）。促性腺激素是两种控制青春期开始、性腺发育和生育能力的激素。此前已知GnRH神经元受其他神经元的调节，但尚不清楚免疫细胞也能影响其功能。

这正是新发现的小胶质细胞功能，小胶质细胞是中枢神经系统中的细胞，负责清除潜在威胁和无用分子。

“发现并非神经元而是免疫细胞的生育调节细胞，这一点非常重要。”CNIO转化与转移小组负责人Eva González-Suárez表示。

该研究表明，小胶质细胞调节GnRH神经元功能的方式是通过表达RANK蛋白。

当CNIO研究小组在动物模型中抑制RANK表达时，雄性和雌性的生殖功能均出现扭曲。在出生时缺乏RANK或在青春期前动物中移除RANK的样本中，性激素水平降低，并出现性腺功能丧失，即性腺功能减退症，这些动物未能进入青春期。当在性成熟的样本中消除RANK后，它们在一个月内变得不育。

一种人类综合征的新突变
为调查RANK是否在人类生育中发挥作用，研究人员对患有先天性促性腺激素缺乏性性腺功能减退症的患者样本进行了基因分析，这是一种与青春期延迟或缺失以及不育症相关的罕见综合征。此前已知该综合征是由GnRH神经元或其产生的分子问题引起的。该研究在部分患者中发现了编码RANK蛋白基因的突变。

作者表示：“这些结果表明，RANK可能成为影响生育能力的内分泌失调和综合征的治疗靶点，同时也是先天性促性腺激素缺乏性性腺功能减退症分子诊断的候选基因。”

González-Suárez强调：“小胶质细胞在调节‘生殖’神经元功能中的作用是全新的发现，并且这种与RANK相关的调节可能发生在其他轴中，以实现其他功能，例如食欲-饱腹轴、应激轴等。”

合作的重要性
作者还希望强调这项研究是跨学科合作重要性的一个范例。Collado解释说：“我的博士论文最初是围绕RANK蛋白在青春期乳腺组织发育中是否发挥作用这个问题展开的。当我们意识到需要探索涉及生育能力、神经元和脑细胞的问题时，我们开始咨询其他领域的同事。”

因此，该团队开始与科尔多瓦大学和科尔多瓦迈蒙尼德斯生物医学研究所（IMIBIC）的Manuel Tena-Sempere、法国国家健康与医学研究所（Inserm）的Vincent Prevot、塞维利亚生物医学研究所（IBiS）的Rafael Fernández Chacón以及瑞士沃州大学医院中心（CHUV）的Nelly Pitteleoud合作。

Collado表示：“我们得出了我们此前无法预见的结论，并掌握了现在可以应用于未来研究的技术和工具。”
